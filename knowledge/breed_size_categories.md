# Breed Size Categories

## Toy and small breeds
Toy and small dog breeds (Chihuahuas, Yorkies, Dachshunds, most cats) generally have faster metabolisms per pound, shorter exercise needs, and often longer lifespans than large breeds, but can be prone to dental issues requiring more frequent dental care tasks.

## Medium breeds
Medium breeds (Beagles, Border Collies, Bulldogs) vary widely in energy level by breed purpose (working/herding breeds need much more activity than companion breeds of similar size).

## Large and giant breeds
Large and giant breeds (Labradors, Great Danes, Mastiffs) mature more slowly, are more prone to joint issues, generally have shorter lifespans, and are more susceptible to bloat, which affects both exercise-after-feeding guidance and long-term joint-care task planning.

## Mixed breeds
For mixed-breed pets, it's reasonable to plan care around the pet's actual observed size and energy level rather than guessing a single "parent breed" profile.
