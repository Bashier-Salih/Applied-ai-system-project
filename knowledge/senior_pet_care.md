# Senior Pet Care

## Adjusted exercise
Senior pets generally benefit from shorter, more frequent activity rather than one long session, to maintain mobility without over-stressing joints. A plan that only schedules one long walk block for a senior dog may be worth flagging for splitting into shorter sessions.

## More frequent wellness checks
Senior pets (roughly 7+ years for medium/large dogs and cats, later for small dogs) are often recommended to have vet wellness checks twice a year instead of once, to catch age-related conditions earlier.

## Medication load
Senior pets are more likely to be on multiple daily medications for chronic conditions, which makes the medication-timing and spacing guidance especially relevant for this age group.

## Mobility and grooming
Arthritic or less mobile senior pets may struggle to groom themselves (especially cats), so increasing brushing frequency can help compensate and also gives an owner a chance to notice new lumps, sores, or weight changes early.
