# Exercise Needs

## Dogs by size and energy level
Small and toy breeds (under 20 lbs) generally need 20-30 minutes of active exercise per day, split across two walks. Medium breeds (20-50 lbs) typically need 30-60 minutes per day. Large and high-energy working/herding breeds (Border Collies, Labradors, German Shepherds) often need 60-90 minutes per day, ideally including off-leash running or fetch, not just leash walking.

## Puppies
Puppies should not be over-exercised while their joints are developing. A common guideline is roughly 5 minutes of structured exercise per month of age, twice a day, until the dog is full grown. Overexertion in puppies under a year old can contribute to joint problems later in life.

## Senior dogs
Senior dogs (typically 7+ years for medium/large breeds, later for small breeds) usually need shorter, gentler, more frequent walks rather than one long walk, to reduce joint strain while still maintaining mobility and weight control.

## Cats
Indoor cats need 10-15 minutes of active play (wand toys, chasing, climbing) once or twice a day to prevent obesity and boredom-related behavior issues. Cats do not need "walks" in the way dogs do, but daily interactive play is still important for physical and mental health.
