# Enrichment and Mental Health

## Why enrichment matters
Mental stimulation (puzzle feeders, training sessions, new walking routes, scent games) reduces boredom-related behavior problems like destructive chewing or excessive vocalizing, and is considered as important as physical exercise for many pets, especially high-energy or working breeds.

## Enrichment for lower-mobility pets
Senior or lower-mobility pets can still get meaningful enrichment from low-impact activities like scent-based games or short training sessions, so enrichment tasks shouldn't automatically be skipped just because a pet's exercise tasks have been reduced.

## Signs of under-stimulation
Repeated destructive behavior, excessive attention-seeking, or pacing can be signs a pet isn't getting enough mental or physical stimulation, which is a pattern worth mentioning even though it isn't a scheduling conflict in the technical sense.
