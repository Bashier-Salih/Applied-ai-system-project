# Puppy and Kitten Care

## Feeding frequency
Puppies and kittens under about 6 months old typically need 3-4 meals a day rather than the 2 meals typical for adults, since their smaller bodies can't store as much energy between meals.

## Socialization and training windows
The period between roughly 3 and 14 weeks of age is considered a key socialization window for puppies; short, frequent positive exposure to new people, sounds, and environments during this window is generally more valuable than occasional long sessions later.

## Vaccination and vet visit scheduling
Puppies and kittens usually need a series of vaccination boosters every 3-4 weeks until about 16 weeks of age. These recurring vet visits are a good candidate for "weekly" or custom-interval recurring tasks rather than daily ones.

## Growth-stage transitions
As puppies and kittens approach adult size (varies by breed, often 9-18 months for dogs depending on size), feeding frequency, portion size, and exercise intensity typically need to be reviewed and adjusted rather than left on the puppy/kitten schedule indefinitely.
