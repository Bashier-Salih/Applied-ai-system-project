# Grooming Frequency

## Coat type and brushing
Short-haired dogs and cats typically need brushing once or twice a week to control shedding. Long-haired or double-coated breeds (Golden Retrievers, Huskies, Persians) often need brushing every day or every other day to prevent matting, especially during seasonal shedding periods.

## Bathing
Most dogs only need a full bath every 4-6 weeks; bathing too frequently can strip natural oils from the skin and coat. Cats are generally self-grooming and rarely need baths at all unless recommended by a vet or groomer for a specific condition.

## Nail trims
Nail trims are typically needed every 3-4 weeks for most dogs and cats, or whenever nails start to click audibly on hard floors. Overdue nail trims can affect gait and comfort over time.

## Professional grooming
Breeds with continuously growing coats (Poodles, Doodles, some terriers) usually need a professional grooming appointment every 4-8 weeks in addition to at-home brushing, to prevent matting and maintain coat health.
