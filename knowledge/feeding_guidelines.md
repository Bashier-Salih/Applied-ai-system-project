# Feeding Guidelines

## Meal frequency
Adult dogs and cats generally do well on two meals a day, spaced roughly 8-12 hours apart. Puppies and kittens under 6 months typically need three to four smaller meals a day to support growth and avoid low blood sugar between meals.

## Consistent timing
Feeding at consistent times each day helps regulate digestion and makes it easier to notice appetite changes, which can be an early sign of illness. Large swings in feeding time from day to day are not dangerous on their own but make it harder to spot when a pet skips a meal.

## Feeding and exercise spacing
For deep-chested large breed dogs (Great Danes, German Shepherds, Standard Poodles), it's a common precaution to avoid vigorous exercise or play within about an hour of a large meal, due to an elevated risk of bloat (gastric dilatation-volvulus). This is less of a concern for small breeds and cats.

## Treats and enrichment feeding
Enrichment activities like puzzle feeders count toward daily food intake and should be factored into total portions, not treated as "extra" food on top of regular meals, to avoid overfeeding.
